Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Biography

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. Known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology, he served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.

Dr. Kalam was a scientist and an engineer who played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development efforts. He worked as a scientist at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).

In addition to his contributions to science and technology, Dr. Kalam was a passionate educator and writer. He inspired millions with his vision for India and his emphasis on education and youth empowerment.

Dr. Kalam received numerous awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor. He passed away on 27 July 2015, leaving behind a legacy of inspiration and achievement.

"You have to dream before your dreams can come true."

Timeline of Major Achievements

  • 1960s: Worked on India's first satellite launch vehicle program.
  • 1983: Appointed as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister.
  • 1998: Instrumental in the successful test of nuclear bombs in Pokhran.
  • 2002: Elected as the 11th President of India.
  • 2007: Completed his term as President and continued to inspire through his work in education and science.

Services and Contributions

  • Development of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle.
  • Advancement of missile technology for national defense.
  • Promotion of science education and innovation among youth.
  • Inspiration for numerous educational and social initiatives.
  • Authored several books on education, science, and leadership.
